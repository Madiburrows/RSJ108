Madi Burrows' How to Create a Successful Image Tweet

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make a successful tweet.

Assembly time: 20 minutes
Awesome rating: 90% awesome

Are you wondering how to increase your skills for using Twitter? It's really simple to understand the basics of Twitter! Follow the steps and you'll become a pro in no time!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Twitter account.
  • Adobe Illustrator.
  • Access to the internet.
  • A positive attitude!
  • Creativity!
  • An open mind!

  1. Brainstorm a tweet that you want to create.

    Look up a keyword in relation to your desired image on websites such as Pexels, Snapstock, Flikr, or Pixabay. These websites are public domain sites that can be used for commercial and personal use. Once you have chosen your image click and drag the photo to your desktop. This will allow for easy access to your photo when you need it in the following steps.

    Get creative when brainstorming for your tweet!

  2. Creating the image for your tweet.

    In Adobe Illustrator, create a new document with dimensions of 1600x900 pixels. Then create the document. After you have your artboard, click and drag the photo of your choice from your desktop, onto the artboard. Once your photo is on the artboard, adjust it to fit the dimensions. Remember to hold “shift” while doing so, so that the photo does not become squished or unevenly shaped. You may also crop the image by right-clicking and pressing “crop.”

  3. Saving your image on Illustrator.

    Once you are happy with the placement and adjustments of your image, it is time to save it as a PNG file. To save an image as a PNG on Adobe Illustrator, go to the top left and find “file.” Click on “file” and then find “Export,” which is towards the bottom. After this, click “Save for web.” When the new screen pops up, find “Name” in the upper right, and change it to “PNG-24.” Finally, press “save” and save the image to your desktop, naming it anything that relates to you, or the image.

  4. Opening twitter.

    Open up Twitter on your computer. Then, click on the “new tweet” icon, located in the middle left of your screen. After this, you will see the template for a new tweet come up. You want to find the “media” icon on the bottom left of the pop up which looks like mountains. This will open your finder, and you want to choose “desktop.” Find the image that you created earlier in Adobe Illustrator. Select the image and “open” it to your tweet.

  5. Composing a "thick tweet".

    Create a tweet that accurately describes the image that you are posting. Make this description as detailed as possible, leaving your audience with no questions about what the image may be about, or why it is being posted. Add hashtags that correlate to the message, as well as the image. Along with hashtags, include relevant handles of those who may have given you feedback, or are related to the overall tweet in some way. Once this is done, post your tweet!
